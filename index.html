UniSAE: Unified Speech Attribute Editing
via Discrete Phonetic Posteriorgrams and
Disentangled Embeddings

Paper(TBA) | Code(TBA) | Dataset(TBA)

Abstract

Speech attribute editing (SAE) aims to flexibly manipulate linguistic content, speaker identity, and emotion within a unified framework. However, existing approaches rely on opaque content units, suffer from emotional data scarcity, and struggle with speaker–emotion entanglement. We propose UniSAE, a unified architecture that integrates an autoregressive transformer for semantic modeling with a diffusion-based acoustic decoder conditioned on speaker and emotion. We introduce Discrete Phonetic PosteriorGram (DPPG), an interpretable, duration-aware representation that enables controllable word-level and phoneme-level editing. To mitigate data scarcity, we construct a large-scale synthetic emotional corpus via zero-shot generation and use it to train the full model. Experiments show that UniSAE generalizes to human-acted emotional speech and achieves strong speaker and emotion controllability with explicit semantic editing capability.
